Overview:

This use-case allows the customer or travel agent to select an itinerary and pay. Once the prices itinerary is displayed the customer can choose to pay with either a credit card of with the award travel miles.

The pay itinerary sequence is divided into 3 diagrams:

(A) Credit Cards - System displays the priced itinerary, customer selects pay with credit card, system returns the list of credit cards on file.
(B) Transmaster - Customer selects the credit card, system sends payment transaction to TransMaster for authorization, system sends confirmation if payment was successful (or failure notification if it failed).
(C) Travel Award - Customer selects award travel, system verifices mileage availabilty, system sends confirmation if travel award payment was successful for the selected itinerary (or failure notification if it failed).

(Note: the failure email alternate flow did not present itself to be significant enough to call for another sequence diagram. A failure notification can be sent just as a confirmation email is sent.)

Assumptions:

  • If the credit card is not authorized, no payment will be attempted.
  • If travel miles do not exist , no payment will be attempted.
  • If agent is making a booking on behalf of the customer , the email will go to both.
  • If the selected seats are no longer available, the transaction will fail.
